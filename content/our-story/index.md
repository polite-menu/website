# The Story Behind Polite Menu

Hi 👋

My name's Daniel.
In 2024 my wife started her dream: [Sweet Loop Cafe](https://sweetloop.ca).

When we were building out the store, I was confident the TVs could display the menus, either from images or the website.
After all, computers have been displaying images for more than 30 years.
"Of course a TV can show an image!" I assured her.

Yes and no.

Smart TVs have or support a web browser, but this approach quickly becomes more pain than it's worth.
For example, the TVs and menus at Sweet Loop are vertical (portrait), but good luck finding a TV that supports portrait mode.
"No problem, just rotate the image", but that is the first step down a rabbit hole of frustrations.

What if you want to rotate multiple images on the same TV?
What if you have menus for certain times of day, like happy hour?
What if you have seasonal (or daily) menu changes?
What if the store WiFi if down?

Commercial displays and signage solve all these problems and more.
But you'll not be surprised to know: commercial solutions cost _a lot_ more.
And to add insult to injury, commercial display software is old, bloated, and complicated.
It's ridiculous; we just want to show an image on a screen!

That's why I created Polite Menu.
It's ridiculously _easy_:
1. Plug device into TV HDMI
2. Activate device
3. Drag-and-drop images

Screen rotation?
Image scheduling?
Offline mode?

All handled. All included in the [ridiculously low price]({{< ref "pricing" >}}).
